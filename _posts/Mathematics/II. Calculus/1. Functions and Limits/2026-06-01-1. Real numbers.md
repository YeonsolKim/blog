**Definition 1.1**

$$
|x| :=
\begin{cases}
x, & x \ge 0,\\
-x, & x < 0.
\end{cases}
$$

<br> 

**Theorem 1.1**

1. $|x| \ge 0$
2. $|xy| = |x|\cdot |y|$
3. $|x+y| \le |x|+|y|$
4. $|x|^2 = x^2$
5. $|x| = 0 \Longrightarrow x = 0$

<br>

**Definition 1.2**

$$
\sqrt{x}=y :\Longleftrightarrow y \ge 0,\ y^2=x
$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.2**

1. $\sqrt{x^2}=|x|$
2. $(\sqrt{x})^2=x$

<br>

**Definition 1.3**

1. For $x+y\ne 0$,

$$
HM(x,y):=\frac{2xy}{x+y}
$$

2. For $xy\ge 0$,

$$
GM(x,y):=\sqrt{xy}
$$

3.

$$
AM(x,y):=\frac{x+y}{2}
$$

4.

$$
QM(x,y):=\sqrt{\frac{x^2+y^2}{2}}
$$

5. For $w_i\ge 0$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{n}w_i=1$,

$$
WAM(x_1,\ldots,x_n):=\sum_{i=1}^{n}w_i x_i
$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.3**

$$
\min(x_1,\ldots,x_n) \le \operatorname{mean}(x_1,\ldots,x_n) \le \max(x_1,\ldots,x_n)
$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.4**

$$
HM \le GM \le AM \le QM
$$
