## 1. Attribution
1. $\langle\text{Object}, \text{Function}\rangle$
2. $\langle\text{Object}, \text{State}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. This key opens the back door.
>2. English is spoken in many countries.<br>
   The data is being processed.<br>
   The road leads to the city center.

## 2. Deontic
1. $\langle\text{Rule}, \text{Content}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. The law forbids smoking in this area.

## 3. Causation
1. $\text{Cause}\rightarrow  \text{Physical Effect}$
2. $\text{Cause}\rightarrow  \text{Perceptual Effect}$

>Examples. 
>1. The cold froze the river.
>2. The music makes me happy.<br>
>   The simple interface disguises the system’s complexity.

## 4. Indication (provide evidence)
1. $\text{Evidence} \rightarrow \text{Indication} \rightarrow \text{Proposition}$
2. $\text{Evidence} \rightarrow \text{Indication} \rightarrow \text{Interpreter}, \text{Interpretation}$

>Examples.
>1. The study suggests that caffeine improves memory.
>2. The features show experts that it is a fish.

## 5. Concept
1. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Definition}\rangle$
2. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Classification}\rangle$
3. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Property}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. A square is a rectangle with four sides of equal length.
>2. Mammalia belongs to the phylum Chordata.
>3. Mercury has a very high surface tension.



## 6. Establishing The Conditions
$\text{Enabler}$: event가 성립할 수 있는 상황적 조건을 마련하는 요소. <br>
$\text{Enabler} \rightarrow \text{Event}$
>Examples.<br>
>This technology allows users to share files instantly.<br>
>The road gives access to the village.


## 7. Formation
$\text{Source of Formation} \rightarrow \text{Formed Result}$
>Examples.<br>
>The environment I grew up in made me who I am today. <br>
>The river formed these deposits.
