## 1. Action
1. $\langle\text{Agent},\text{Act}, \text{Theme}\rangle$ *$\text{Agent}$는 의지를 가지고 동작을 수행하는 주체
2. $\langle\text{Agent},\text{Act},\text{Patient}\rangle$ *$\text{Patient}$는 단순 대상이 아니라 내부적으로 상태가 변화하는 대상
3. $\langle\text{Agent},\text{Act},\text{Proposition}\rangle$

> Examples.
> 1. The company exported rice to Japan.
> 2. He ate the apple.
   She painted the wall.
> 3. The teacher recommended that she be sent home.

## 2. Experience
1. $\langle\text{Experiencer},\text{Experience},\text{Stimulus}\rangle$ *의지적 작용이 아니라 심리적, 감각적 인지를 경험하는 주체
2. $\langle\text{Experiencer},\text{Experience},\text{Proposition}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. I feel cold.
   She loves music.
>2. I believe she will come home.

## 3. Attribution
1. $\langle\text{Object}, \text{Function}\rangle$
2. $\langle\text{Object}, \text{State}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. This key opens the back door.
>2. I was hospitalized.
   English is spoken in many countries.
   The data is being processed.
   The road leads to the city center.

## 4. Deontic
1. $\langle\text{Rule}, \text{Constraint},\text{Situation}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. The law forbids smoking in this area.

## 5. Causation
1. $\langle\text{Cause}, \text{Effect}\rangle$

>Examples. 
>1. The cold froze the river.
   The music makes me happy.

## 6. Indication
1. $\langle\text{Evidence}, \text{Indication}, \text{Proposition}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. The study suggests that caffeine improves memory.

## 7. Conceptual
1. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Definition}\rangle$
2. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Classification}\rangle$
3. $\langle\text{Term}, \text{Property}\rangle$

>Examples.
>1. A square is a rectangle with four sides of equal length.
>2. Mammalia belongs to the phylum Chordata.
>3. Mercury has a very high surface tension.
