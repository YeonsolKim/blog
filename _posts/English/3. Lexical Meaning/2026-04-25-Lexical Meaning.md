<b>determine</b><sup>2</sup>: ascertain exactly, as a result of research or calculation<br>
<b>examine</b>: inspect "in detail"; investigate "thoroughly"<br>
<b>investigate</b>: carry out a systematic inquiry "to discover the facts of an incident"<br>
<b>look into</b>: explore the facts about something<br>
<br>

<b>method</b> (noun): a particular form of procedure for accomplishing something<br>
<b>style</b>: a way that is "characteristic of particular period or person, etc.<br>
<br>

<b>religion</b>: "the belief and worship" of superhuman powers or gods<br>
<b>religious</b>: relating to religion<br>
<b>custom</b>: a way of doing something that is specific to a particular society<br>
<b>tradition</b>: a "long-established" custom that has been passed on from one generation to another<br>
<b>traditional</b>: existing as a part of tradition<br>
<b>society</b>: the aggregate of people living together<br>
<b>traditional society</b>: long-established society concerned with tradition<br>
<b>movement</b><sup>2</sup>: a campaign undertaken by a broad group of people working together to advance their social or political ideas<br>
<b>campaign</b>: organized actions carried out to achive a particular goal<br>
<br>

<b>typical</b>: having the distinctive qualities "of a particular type"<br>
<b>typically</b>: in a way that is characteristic "of a particular type"<br>
<b>hallmark</b>: a distinctive feature of a particular type<br>
<br>

<b>modest</b><sup>1</sup>: unassuming in the estimation of one's abilities or achievments<br>
<b>moderate</b>: average in amout or intensity, etc.<br>
<b>modest</b><sup>2</sup>: subjectively moderate; small<br>
<br>

<b>remorse</b>: deep regret for a wrong committed<br>
<br>

<b>conflict</b><sup>1</sup>: a protracted argument<br>
<b>conflict</b><sup>2</sup>: a condition in which a person experiences a clash of opposing wishes<br>
<b>conflict</b><sup>3</sup>: a prolonged "armed struggle"<br>
<br>

<b>depart from</b>: deviate from a traditional course<br>
<br>

<b>abnormal</b>: deviating from what is normal<br>
<b>unreasonable</b>: not based on good sense<br>
<b>wildly</b>: to an extreme degree; in an excessive and surprising way<br>
<b>absurd</b><sup>1</sup>: wildly unreasonable and illogical<br>
<b>outrageous</b>: extremely shocking, in a way that causes anger<br>
<br> 

<b>argue</b><sup>2</sup>: exchange diverging views<br>
<b>debate</b><sup>2</sup>: argue<sup>2</sup> about, in a formal<sup>1</sup> manner<br>
<b>authority</b><sup>2</sup>: a person or organization having power<br>
<b>official</b>: relating to an authority or public body<br>
<b>formal</b><sup>1</sup>: done in accordance with rules of conventions or etiquette<br>
<b>formal</b><sup>2</sup>: conccerned with outward form or appearance, as distinct from content<br>
<b>distinct</b>: recognizably different and clearly seperate<br>
<b>recognize</b> [rekəgnaiz]: identify someone or something as already known<br>
<b>perceive</b>: become aware of something through the senses<br>
<b>resemble</b> [rizembəl]
<br>

<b>ascertain</b> [æsərtein]: ditermine<sup>2</sup> somethiing with certainty<br>
<b>confirm</b>: establish correctness of something previously suspected to be the case<br>
<br>

<b>moral</b>: concerned with the principles of right and wrong of human<br>
<b>just</b><sup>1</sup> (adj.): "behaving" according to what is morally right<br>
<b>justice</b>: just behavior or treatment<br>
<b>treatment</b>: the manner in which someone deals with someone or something<br>
<br>

<b>abominable</b>: causing moral revulsion<br>
<b>revulsion</b>: a sense of disgust<br>
<b>uprising</b> (n.): an act of resistance<br>
<br>

<b>revoke</b>: put an end to the "validity" of<br>
<b>repeal</b>: revoke a law<br>
<b>abrogate</b> [ebrəgeit]: repeal or do away with<br>
<br>

<b>practice</b><sup>2</sup>: the "actual" application of an idea<br>
<b>discipline</b> [disəsplin]: the practice of training people to obey rules<br>
<b>enforce</b>: compel<sup>2</sup> observance of a law or rule<br>
<b>compel</b><sup>1</sup>: force someone to do something<br>
<b>compel</b><sup>2</sup>: bring about something by the use of force<br>
<b>bring about</b>: cause something to happen<br>
<br>

<b>hurriedly</b>: in a rushed manner<br>
<b>secret</b> (adj.): not known or not meant to be known by others<br>
<b>secretly</b>: in a secret way<br>
<b>abscond</b> [əbˈskənd]: leave hurriedly and secretly<br>
<b>accentuate</b> [ekˈsen.tʃu.eit]: make more noticeable<br>
<b>access</b> [ˈek.ses] (n.): the right or opportunity to use or approach something<br>
<b>abundant</b> [əˈbən.dənt]<br>
<b>abuse</b> [əbjus]: use something to bad effect<br>
<br>

<b>restore</b>: return to a former condition<br>
<br>

<b>liberal</b><sup>1</sup>: willing to respect opinion new and different from one's own<br>
<b>liberal</b><sup>2</sup>: relating to a political and social philosophy that promotes individual right<br>
<b>conservative</b>: favoring socially traditional ideas (contrasted with liberal<sup>1</sup>)<br>

<b>primarily</b> [praimerəli]: as the chief or central thing rather than a secondary one<br>
<b>mostly</b>: to a great extent<br>
<b>largely</b>: mostly, but not completely<br>
<br>

<b>class</b><sup>1</sup>: a social division<sup>2</sup> based on social or economic status<br>
<b>division</b><sup>2</sup>: each of the parts into which something is divided<br>
<b>category</b>: a division in a system of classification<br>
<b>type</b>: a person or thing that has the character of a category<br>
<br>

<b>set</b>: a group of things that belongs together<br>
<b>class</b><sup>2</sup>: a set of things "having some property in common"<br>
